Clash Royale Emote Guide

In Clash Royale, emotes can play a major role in your ability to win, as long as you use them correctly. This guide will help you to use emotes correctly, in order to help you to win as much as possible. There are multiple different tactics which you can use, including spamming and winding up based on plays.

The Beginning of the game

In the beginning of the game, you are limited in your ability to wind up the opponent, as nothing has happened yet to be the subject of your mocking. However, there are some things you can do which will immediately set the tone of the match, and make your opponent realise the toxicity of the match to come. This will alter how they think, and potentially make them play worse.

Mid Game

The midgame is where you can really let your emotes go wild, as there are many, many ways to enrage your opponent. For example, one of my personal favourite emotes is the princess yawning, as it can be used in a wide variety of situations to maximal effectiveness, such as when you take a tower, or easily defend a push.

During this period, you will find that your opponent will play worse, and worse, and worse, as long as you are emoting to maximal effect. Becuase of this, you will find more and more opportunities arise with which you can exploit your wide range of toxic emotes to almost guarantee a win.
